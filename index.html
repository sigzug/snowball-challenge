What happens to unsold clothes?

Incinerating them to dust

The clothes in the stores that don’t get sold will most likely be incinerated. Some companies choose to burn the clothes in a way to make electricity back from them. This has been a step in the right direction. There was an incident with a British company Burbary in 2018 that was busted on burning a total amount of almost $40 million. It became an outrage in the public and the parliament demanded a change. And they did change. But sadly they were not the only ones.

Who is doing it?

Sadly there's no scarcity of the practice of burning clothes in the world's companies. Companies from Louis Vuitton to Nike have all been burning clothes in many years. The worst part about it, is they mostly do it not because the clothes are longer profitable, but to maintain exclusivity in the brand by making it more hard to find. But it’s not only the exclusive companies. H&M were busted by danish TV for having burned 60 tons of clothing since 2013, both old and new. There are many more. The fashion industry has been one of the world's biggest polluters for a long time, and this is one of the practices that could help.but what can they and we do?

This is what we can do

Our solution is to keep the donations going. There is clearly a lack of motivation for the companies to keep giving away. We want the donation process to be so simple and easy that it’s embarrassing for the companies. We would also need to keep pressure on the companies through media by always demanding something from them.
