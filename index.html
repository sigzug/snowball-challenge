What happens to unsold clothes?

Incinerating to dust

The clothes in stores that don’t get sold will most likely be incinerated. Some companies choose to burn the clothes in power plants to make electricity back from them. And that has been a step in the right direction, but it doesn’t make up even a tiny bit of all the energy used to make the clothes. There was an incident with a British company called Burbary in 2018. They were busted on burning a total amount of almost $40 million worth in clothes. An outrage sweeped over the public and the parliament where pushed to demand a change from the company. This did eventually lead to them stopping the practice, but sadly they were the one of thousands.

A black chanel bag

Who is doing it?

Sadly there's no scarcity of the practice of burning clothes in the world's companies. Companies from Louis Vuitton to Nike have all been burning clothes in many years and don’t show any sign of stopping. The worst part about it is that they mostly do it to maintain exclusivity in the brand. They don’t do it because their storages are full, it’s just to keep an image. But it’s not only the exclusive and expensive companies that do this though. H&M were busted by danish TV for having burned 60 tons of clothing since 2013, and it was both old and new clothes. After the first news articles came out about this practice, countless whistleblowers have stepped forward to the public in a lot of companies. The fashion industry has been one of the world's biggest polluters for a long time now, and this is one major reason for them being one of the worst. What can we do to help them make better and more environmental choices for the clothes they are throwing away?

This is what we can do

Our solution is to keep the donations going. There is clearly a lack of motivation for the companies to keep giving away. We want the donation process to be so simple and easy that it’s embarrassing for the companies. We would also need to keep pressure on the companies through media by always demanding something from them.
